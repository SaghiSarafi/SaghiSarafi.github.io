Brief Bio

I am a PhD student in Geography at the University of New Mexico, specializing in spatial analysis, remote sensing, and urban sustainability. My research focuses on leveraging geospatial technologies to analyze urban heat islands, environmental monitoring, and transportation planning.

Previously, I worked as a researcher at the Carinthia University of Applied Sciences, where I contributed to building footprint digitization projects in Austria. I also conducted research at the University of New Mexico, utilizing remote sensing technology to analyze urban heat islands across various cities. My professional experience includes serving as a spatial data analyst at Dynamic Kariz System Design and Architecture Company in Tehran, Iran, where I was involved in developing national geolocation and navigation systems. Additionally, I worked as an urban development engineer at Naghshine Mandegar Engineering Office, overseeing construction projects. I have technical expertise in Google Earth Engine, Python, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, PostgreSQL, Node.js, and Leaflet, along with GIS platforms like ArcGIS and QGIS, and urban design tools such as AutoCAD and CityEngine. My specialization includes geospatial data processing, remote sensing analysis, and developing web-based mapping applications.

I hold an MSc in Spatial Information Management from Carinthia University of Applied Sciences, an MSc in Urban Design from Yazd University, and a BSc in Urban Planning from University of Kurdistan.
